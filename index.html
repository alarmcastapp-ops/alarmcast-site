Built around podcasts

The podcast is the point

Set it once and wake to the latest episode of a show you actually want to hear. Everything around it is built to make that effortless.

Wake to the latest episode

Pick a show once. Every morning Alarmcast plays its newest episode, downloaded overnight so it starts the instant the alarm fires, even with no signal.

A curated catalogue

Browse hand-picked categories, News, Comedy, Business, True Crime, Tech and more, or search the full index. Finding the right show takes seconds.

Your Library, one tap to assign

Subscribe to the shows you love and point any alarm at one. Each shows "Next: Latest episode" so you always know what is queued.

Latest, or carry on

Wake to the very latest episode, or continue the one you were part-way through. The choice is per show, set once and then forgotten.

Line up a follow-up

Add a second podcast that plays after the first episode ends, so a quick news brief can roll straight into the show you really want.

Never silent

Lapsed subscription, deleted show, no network? A warm birdsong chime always takes over. You never oversleep to silence.

A gentle bonus

Gentle Wake eases you in

Not a morning person? Switch on Gentle Wake and soft birdsong fades in before the alarm, then hands over to your podcast at the set time. A calmer start, layered on an alarm that still always fires.

Reliability first

The alarm fires every time

Other podcast alarms feel clever until the morning one quietly fails to go off. Alarmcast is built around one rule: it wakes you up, no matter what Android does.

How it works

From podcast to alarm in three steps

1

Find your show

Search the Discover tab, preview episodes, and subscribe to the podcasts you want in your Library.

2

Set your alarm

Choose a time and the days, then attach a channel. Tune volume, snooze, fade-in and a reminder lead time per alarm.

3

Wake up informed

When the alarm goes off, it plays the latest episode straight from local storage, so it starts instantly. You wake up already caught up.

Why there's a Pro listener plan

We got the core working first: the part that has to fire every morning. Then we wanted more for ourselves. We fall behind on long episodes and want to pick them back up days later. We want to skip around, or jump to another episode while the alarm is still ringing, and line up a second show after the first.

That was the hardest part of the app to build. Doing live episode controls on top of an alarm that is already going off, without ever risking the wake-up, took months of work and a lot of rewrites. The Pro listener plan is where that work lives. If you spend as much time in your feed as we do, it is for you.
